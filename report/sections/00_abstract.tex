We study whether augmenting the Adaptive Biasing Force (ABF) method with a
Fisher--Rao (FR) birth--death correction improves the computation of a
reaction-coordinate free energy, and, if so, how the correction must be
constructed. We test this on \emph{three} systems of increasing difficulty, each
with an essentially exact reference: a two-dimensional double-well
\emph{metastability} model (quadrature-exact reference); a two-dimensional
\emph{entropic-bottleneck} model with a narrow transverse channel, for which both
the reference free energy and the conditional law $Y\mid X=x\sim\mathcal
N(0,1/(\beta\omega(x)^2))$ are analytic; and a many-body condensed-phase
\emph{Weeks--Chandler--Andersen (WCA) dimer} in a dense solvent
(thermodynamic-integration reference, GPU). In each case the FR step reallocates
walkers along the reaction coordinate by killing replicas in over-represented
regions and cloning whole configurations from under-represented regions, nudging
the empirical marginal $\phat_t$ toward a target $q_t$. We compare ABF only against
ABF+FR with an \emph{estimated}, \emph{uniform}, or \emph{oracle} target, on
matched seeds. The central finding is that \textbf{FR helps ABF in proportion to
how sample-starved ABF is}: a modest convergence accelerator --- and occasionally
mildly harmful --- where plain ABF already samples well (metastability,
$+\EstIntegImpPct\%$ integrated error; hot bottleneck, $\EBwarmGainBetaFour\%$),
but a large and reliable win where ABF is starved (cold bottleneck,
$+\EBgainStrong\%$, $\EBcoldWin/20$ seeds; WCA dimer, $+\WCAtunedGainPct\%$,
$\WCAtunedWin/10$ seeds, the relative gain growing with simulation budget and the
largest absolute error removed in the densest solvent). The dominant
mechanism is \emph{balanced-resampling variance reduction}, not free-energy-shape
steering: in the starved regimes a uniform target nearly matches the estimated one
and an oracle target (which is allowed to use the reference $\Fref$, and is never a
deployable method) is no better --- or, in the entropic bottleneck, \emph{worse}
than ABF. The one failure mode is diversity collapse under too-aggressive
birth--death (the WCA dimer's gain reverses once \texttt{fr\_rate} is pushed well
past its sweet spot), bounded by how
fast the system's orthogonal degrees of freedom re-equilibrate cloned replicas.
Across all successful settings FR preserves the conditional law $Y\mid X=x$ on
which ABF depends --- confirmed directly against the analytic conditional of the
bottleneck model.
